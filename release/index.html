VOCALOID

You can't fight progress, no matter how strange it sounds.
—Miriam Stockley

Initial announcement and release of the first VOCALOIDs.

2003

  • February 26: YAMAHA announces VOCALOID ({% include jpn.html jpn="ボーカロイド" rom="BOOKAROIDO" %}), a new vocal synthesizing software.

2004

  • January 15: LE♂N and L♀LA, a pair of Zero-G VOCALOIDs based upon soul singers, are released as the first VOCALOIDs.
  • July: MIRIAM, a Zero-G VOCALOID based upon the singer Miriam Stockley, is released.
  • November 5: The first Japanese VOCALOID, MEIKO ({% include jpn.html jpn="メイコ" rom="MEIKO" %}), is released through Crypton.

2006

  • February: KAITO ({% include jpn.html jpn="カイト" rom="KAITO" %}), MEIKO's counterpart, is released through Crypton.
  • December 6: The Japan-based video sharing site Nico Nico Douga ({% include jpn.html jpn="ニコニコ|動|画" furi="ニコニコ|どう|が" rom="NIKONIKO douga" %}) is launched.

mothy's Beginnings

Until that time, it’s a love of just ten minutes.
—Ten Minute Love

The release of VOCALOID2 and mothy's first few years as a producer.

2007

  • January: YAMAHA announces VOCALOID2, a newer version of the VOCALOID software.
  • June 29: Sweet ANN is released by PowerFX as the first VOCALOID2.
  • August 31: Hatsune Miku ({% include jpn.html jpn="初|音|ミク" furi="はつ|ね|ミク" rom="hatsune MIKU" %}) is released by Crypton, quickly gaining popularity in Japan through Nico Nico Douga, with {% include jpn.html jpn="mothy" furi="モッチー" rom="MOCCHII" %} also being part of the excitement.
  • December 3: Crypton launches the website PIAPRO ({% include jpn.html jpn="ピアプロ" rom="PIAPURO" %}) for Vocaloid-related community creations.
  • December 27: Kagamine Rin & Len ({% include jpn.html jpn="鏡|音|リン|・|レン" furi="かがみ|ね|リン|・|レン" rom="kagamine RIN · REN" %}), a dual voicebank consisting of complementary girl & boy vocals, is released by Crypton.
  • mothy chooses to buy Kagamine Rin & Len instead of Hatsune Miku due to her overwhelming popularity at the time.
